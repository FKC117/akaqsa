Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from prospective families about our Islamic educational approach

Our curriculum seamlessly weaves Islamic principles throughout all subjects while meeting and exceeding state standards. For example, in mathematics, we explore Islamic geometric patterns and the mathematical concepts in Quranic verses. In science, we study Allah's creation with wonder and gratitude. Our students consistently perform above grade level on standardized assessments while developing strong Islamic character.

We welcome children at all levels of Islamic knowledge! Our teachers are skilled at meeting each child where they are and providing appropriate support. We offer differentiated Islamic studies instruction and additional support for students new to Arabic or Quranic studies. Many of our most successful students started with little to no Islamic educational background.

Our graduates are well-prepared for both Islamic high schools and secular institutions. We provide rigorous academic preparation, critical thinking skills, and strong Islamic identity formation. Our alumni regularly receive acceptance to top high schools and maintain their Islamic values throughout their educational journey. We also offer transition counseling and ongoing support for graduates.

We use positive, Islamic-based behavior management rooted in prophetic teachings about kindness, patience, and wisdom. Our approach emphasizes natural consequences, restorative justice, and character development rather than punitive measures. We work closely with families to ensure consistency between home and school expectations, always treating each child with dignity and respect.

We believe parents are their child's first teachers and value strong home-school partnerships. While we don't require specific volunteer hours, we offer many opportunities for involvement including classroom assistance, Islamic event planning, fundraising, and educational workshops. We understand that families have different capacities for involvement and work with each family's unique circumstances.
